## Executive Summary

Ditis is a tech-enabled treasure hunting startup that applies a proprietary data intelligence platform — **Ditis Core** — to the discovery, validation, and recovery of lost valuables worldwide. We combine autonomous drone systems (aerial, marine, terrestrial), advanced sensor arrays, and an AI-driven data fusion pipeline to systematically identify, prioritize, and recover high-value targets at a fraction of historical cost.

Our thesis: **the treasure hunting industry has been limited not by the existence of targets, but by the cost and inefficiency of finding them.** Advances in autonomous systems, remote sensing, and machine learning have collapsed that cost curve. Salvage Group is built to exploit that inflection point.

## Part 1: Strategy

### 1.1 Mission

To become the world's leading technology-driven treasure recovery firm by building a proprietary intelligence platform that turns historical data, environmental signals, and sensor feeds into actionable expedition targets — and executing on those targets with capital efficiency and legal precision.

**“Quod est deorsum, hoc est ad divitias.”**
*(That which is below, belongs to the riches.)*

#### I. The Name

In the ancient Roman rite, **Dis Pater** was the "Wealthy Father"—the god of the deep earth, the mineral veins, and the hidden gems of the underworld. He was not a god of death, but a god of **subsurface abundance**. To the ancients, the greatest wealth was always *chthonic*: it lay beneath the visible world, waiting for the systematic effort of the seeker.

**Ditis** is the possessive form. It signifies that which *belongs* to the Wealthy Father. We chose this name because we do not merely "find" treasure; we identify the assets that have fallen out of human circulation and into the domain of Ditis. We are the stewards of the recovery process.

#### II. The Core Anthesis

For centuries, the recovery of lost history has been a gamble—a domain of "rogue divers" and "fortune hunters." This era is over. The limitation of the treasure hunting industry was never a lack of targets; it was a **poverty of vision.** The sea and the soil have acted as a natural encryption for the world's lost capital. Until now, the cost to decrypt these locations exceeded the value within. **Ditis Inc.** was founded on a singular realization: *Advances in autonomous systems, probabilistic intelligence, and remote sensing have finally collapsed the cost of discovery.*

#### III. The Instrument: Ditis Specula

If Ditis is the father of the deep, **Ditis Core** is his Eye.

Our platform—The Watchtower—does not guess. It fuses centuries of archival shadows, environmental permutations, and multi-spectral sensor feeds into a single, probabilistic truth. Where others see a vast, empty ocean, Specula sees a high-fidelity map of historical anomalies. We do not "search"; we **verify.**

#### IV. The Architecture of the Vault

We operate with the cold precision of an intelligence agency and the risk-adjusted discipline of a venture fund.

* **The Vault (Ditis Inc.):** The immutable fortress of our Intellectual Property.
* **The Shield (Operating Subsidiaries):** The frontline units that execute in the physical world.
* **The Syndicate (Expedition Funds):** The mechanism by which we deploy capital into the deep.

#### V. The Mandate

We seek the **Merchant Royal**, the **1715 Fleet**, and the **Flor de la Mar** not merely for the gold, but for the **alpha**. We are building a compounding data moat—a digital ledger of the world's lost wealth. Every drone flight, every archival scan, and every successful recovery feeds the engine.

We are the sole developers of a new era. We are the bridge between the analog past and the autonomous future.

**Ditis is the Father of Riches. We are the ones who have learned to speak his language.**

### 2.4 Fusion Engine

The Fusion Engine is where Ditis Core transforms raw data into intelligence:

#### 2.4.1 Entity Resolution & Knowledge Graph

- Every ingested record is decomposed into **entities**: ships, people, locations, dates, cargo items, events.
- Entities are resolved across sources (e.g., "Nuestra Señora de Atocha" in a manifest, "the Atocha wreck" in a newspaper, coordinates in a sonar scan — all linked to one canonical entity).
- Relationships are stored in a **knowledge graph**: Ship → carried → Cargo; Ship → sank at → Location; Location → surveyed by → Sensor Pass.

#### 2.4.2 Probabilistic Target Scoring

Each candidate target receives a composite **Score** (0–100) based on weighted factors:

| Factor | Weight | Inputs |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Historical Confidence** | 25% | Number of independent archival sources confirming existence and approximate location |
| **Value Estimate** | 20% | Cargo manifests, insurance records, comparable auction results |
| **Location Precision** | 20% | How tightly can we constrain the search box? (archival + drift models + sensor data) |
| **Legal Feasibility** | 15% | Clarity of jurisdiction, permit availability, ownership risk |
| **Recovery Difficulty** | 10% | Depth, environmental conditions, required equipment |
| **Sensor Validation** | 10% | Has any sensor pass returned a positive anomaly at the predicted location? |

Scores update dynamically as new data enters the system (e.g., a successful sonar pass jumps the Sensor Validation score; a new legal dispute drops Legal Feasibility).

#### 2.4.3 AI/ML Model Suite

| Model | Purpose | Training Data |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| **NLP/NER Pipeline** | Extract entities (ships, cargo, locations, dates) from unstructured text | Annotated maritime/historical corpus |
| **Sonar Anomaly Classifier** | Distinguish wreck signatures from geological features in sonar imagery | Labeled sonar datasets (known wrecks vs. false positives) |
| **Drift & Decay Model** | Predict current position of objects given sinking location, currents, sediment transport | Oceanographic models + known recovery positions |
| **Burial/Exposure Predictor** | Forecast when storm or tidal cycles will expose buried targets | Historical weather + sediment data + known exposure events |
| **Value Estimator** | Estimate potential recovery value from cargo type, quantity, condition | Auction databases + insurance records |
| **Search Area Optimizer** | Given uncertainty in location, optimize drone survey grid for max coverage / min cost | Monte Carlo simulations on location probability distributions |

### 2.5 Decision Layer

#### Target Dashboard

- Real-time ranked list of all candidate targets with Salvage Scores.
- Drill-down into any target: knowledge graph view, source documents, sensor data overlays, legal status, financial model.
- Filter and sort by region, score, value, legal status, required capability.

#### Expedition Planner

- For any target, auto-generate a preliminary expedition plan: required equipment, estimated cost, timeline, permitting steps, team requirements.
- Monte Carlo simulation of expedition ROI given probability distributions on value and recovery success.
- Stage-gate workflow: Research → Survey → Validation → Recovery → Monetization, with go/no-go criteria at each gate.

#### GIS Map Interface

- All targets plotted on a global map with heat-map overlays for target density and score.
- Layer toggle: historical shipping routes, known wreck fields, legal jurisdiction boundaries, sensor coverage, environmental conditions.
- Draw a search area → Ditis Core returns all known targets within it, ranked.

#### Investor Portal

- Per-expedition financial model: capital required, expected value, probability-weighted return, timeline.
- Portfolio-level view: diversification across geographies, risk tiers, and expedition stages.
- Automated reporting: expedition status updates, milestone tracking, spend vs. budget.
